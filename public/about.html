About Timeliner

Movation

In "Societies of the World 14: The British Empire", we saw two contrasting methods of historical understanding. We saw the British Empire from a top-down perspective: the wars and rebellions, the decisions made by political leaders and kings, and the larger-than-life economic forces that drove the makings of the largest empire in history. At the same time, Professor Jasanoff emphasized the bottom-up perspective; we strove to understand how the common person experienced empire, as well as how cultural and social forces could shape as well as be shaped by empire.


Timeliner is designed to assist with this second perspective on history. It gives users the chance to immerse themselves in the sights, sounds, and locations of empire, serving the same purpose as the digital exercises in SW 14. It is particularly suitable for deepening one's understanding of the journeys and experience of individual people - as Professor Jasanoff mentioned, "Men On the Spot" were incredibly important to the British Empire, when communication was slow and locations were remote. I have chosen to explore the journeys of three European explorers through Africa to demonstrate the potential of this web app.

Details

This website was build using ruby on rails, along with the google maps API. It currently allows creation of timelines that incorporate images, youtube videos, gps coordinates, and text description. Unfortunately, right now, only the owner of the timeline may submit events to it, but after the completion of my final project, multi-user collaboration on timelines will be enabled. It is designed to be an alternative to the "Digital Exercises" tool that was used in SW14; I will continue to polish it during the summer.
